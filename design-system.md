# M&M Meat Processing Design System

## Brand Direction

The visual direction is **refined local utility**: clean, airy, trustworthy, and grounded in craftsmanship instead of luxury for luxury's sake.

This site should feel:
- spacious
- editorial
- useful
- local
- calm and confident
- down to earth

This site should not feel:
- overly masculine or aggressive
- dark and heavy
- corporate
- gimmicky
- rustic in a cliche way
- like SaaS with different nouns

## Color Palette

### Core

- `Paper`: `#F7F1E8`
- `Paper Strong`: `#FFFAF4`
- `Ink`: `#1F1714`
- `Ink Soft`: `#3E322C`
- `Muted`: `#6F6259`
- `Line`: `#D6C7B8`

### Accent

- `Crimson Oxblood`: `#6F2D27`
- `Warm Brass`: `#B38752`

### Usage

- Use `Paper` and `Paper Strong` for the main backgrounds.
- Use `Ink` for headings, important UI, and strong calls to action.
- Use `Muted` for supporting copy and navigation.
- Use `Crimson Oxblood` sparingly for emphasis, eyebrows, selection, and high-value accents.
- Use `Warm Brass` as a secondary warmth note in gradients, glows, or separators.

## Competitive Note

The closest useful reference from `goodranchers.com` is not the ecommerce styling. It is the clarity of:
- immediate trust signals
- strong section hierarchy
- obvious next steps

For M&M, we should borrow that clarity while avoiding:
- subscription-box energy
- overly polished national-brand language
- crowded merchandising layouts

## Typography

### Primary Pairing

- `Newsreader`
  Why: elegant, editorial, high-end without feeling flashy
  Use for: headlines, section titles, brand moments

- `Manrope`
  Why: modern, highly legible, clean enough to support an airy layout
  Use for: paragraphs, navigation, buttons, labels, supporting UI

### Alternate Font Pairings To Consider Later

- `Bodoni Moda` + `Instrument Sans`
- `Cormorant Garamond` + `Hanken Grotesk`
- `Fraunces` + `Manrope`

## Layout Principles

- Prefer wide margins and large vertical spacing.
- Keep text blocks readable with controlled measure.
- Use cards sparingly and only when grouping information improves scanning.
- Let the serif headlines carry most of the personality.
- Keep sections easy to understand at a glance.
- Build the homepage around real customer flow, not generic marketing sections.

## Components

### Buttons

- Primary button: dark `Ink` background with light text
- Secondary button: transparent or soft paper background with subtle border
- Shape: pill or soft rounded rectangle

### Cards

- Light paper-based surfaces
- Fine border
- Low, soft shadow
- Rounded corners, but not overly bubbly

### Navigation

- Simple, minimal, and lightly framed
- Avoid oversized sticky headers unless needed later

## Motion

- Keep motion restrained and smooth.
- Focus on hover polish and page-load presence rather than flashy effects.
- If we add scroll animations later, they should support hierarchy, not distract from it.

## Photography Guidance

- Prioritize real facility, product, or local-process imagery over stock when available.
- Use close crops, texture, and detail.
- Avoid overly dramatic dark overlays.
- If stock is temporarily needed, choose clean, high-resolution editorial imagery with neutral backgrounds.

## Writing Tone

- Plainspoken
- Reliable
- Informative
- Confident
- Neighborly

Avoid generic marketing claims. Prefer specifics about service, process, care, and locality.

## Homepage Priorities

- Speak to both families and hunters.
- Help people understand where to click next.
- Reduce confusion before a phone call.
- Keep the first screen direct and grounded.
- Make the whole experience feel cleaner than the current site without becoming sterile.

## Next Expansion Ideas

- Service detail pages for `Custom Processing` and `Venison Processing`
- Downloadable or interactive cut sheets
- FAQ section for order prep and pickup
- Seasonal notices or booking callouts handled in a more current, maintainable way
